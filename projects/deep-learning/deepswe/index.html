TL;DR: you can read the paper here (Italian) and check the repository.

Between late 2020 and summer 2021, I had the pleasure to work with Prof. Alessandro Dal Pal√∫ and the HyLab research team at the University of Parma. The subject for this thesis project is Shallow Water Equations and methods in deep learning to compute them with cost and time efficiency.

My proposal was an initial approach to video prediction models: the flooding scenario is discretized into a 2D grid with variable resolution. Such matrix contains information about the boundaries (incoming flows) and a flooding is represented by a sequence of frames, a video. In its simplest version, the model proposed is an autoregressive autoencoder with ConvLSTM blocks. The purpose was to demostrate the possibility to obtain acceptable predictions and to explore the limits of this solution.

This framework aims to structure this work of research and provide a toolset for further progress. The dataset has been generated with the fluid-dynamics simulator PARFLOOD, you can find further details about data pre-processing and theoretical foundations here (in italian!).
