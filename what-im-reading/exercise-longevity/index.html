Exercise and longevity (Gremeaux et al. 2012)

29. December 2021

This paper was written by the Cardiovascular Prevention and Rehabilitation Center (Centre √âPIC) in Montreal, Canada, in collaboration with CHU Poitiers, CHU Dijon and the University of Poitiers in France.

Longevity, the ability to reach an age above average, is influenced by intrinsic (genetic) and extrinsic factors (lifestyle, nutrition, mental and physical wellbeing). In this paper, the authors suggest there is a correlation between exercise and reduction in risks for cardiovascular diseases, various forms of cancer and decaying in muscles and the skeleton; moreover, such correlation may be proportional to the exercise volume (duration x intensity).

Fig.1. ‚ÄúViciouscircle‚Äù of inactivity and positive effects of the regular physical activity.

One paragraph I retain important is the definition of exercise. The “perception of performance loss” has to possible consequences:

  • Exercise stimulation, leading to performance enhancement and thus well being (virtuous circle)
  • Physical inactivity, leading to muscle wasting and cardiovascular decaying (vicious circle)

This could possibly reflect the truth. It is safe to say that exercise has positive effects on health, though the difficulty seems to lie in building a virtuous circle. Changing one’s habits or “hacking the routine” is not simple. Simple, atomic activites can help for self improvement: daily journaling, setting simple achievable goals.
